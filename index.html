Information about football

Football, also known as soccer, is a popular sport played worldwide. It involves two teams of eleven players each, trying to score goals by kicking a ball into the opposing team's net. The game is played on a rectangular field with a goal at each end. Football requires skill, teamwork, and physical fitness. It is watched by millions of fans and has produced some of the greatest athletes in history. The World Cup, held every four years, is the most prestigious football tournament.

Offside

Offside is a rule in football that prevents attacking players from being in an offside position when the ball is played to them by a teammate. A player is considered offside if they are closer to the opposing team's goal than both the ball and the second-to-last defender, at the moment the ball is played. If a player is offside, the opposing team is awarded a free-kick. The offside rule is designed to prevent players from gaining an unfair advantage by standing in an offside position and waiting for the ball to be played to them.
